# Google Play ASO And Conversion

Use when the user asks for Google Play ASO, ranking, downloads, Play listing quality, conversion, custom store listings, experiments, or launch growth.

Keep this as a growth pass, not a submission blocker, unless metadata is misleading, policy-risky, or inconsistent with app behavior.

## Levers

- Title: 30 characters. Highest metadata weight. Use brand plus credible high-intent descriptor when natural.
- Short description: 80 characters. Strong value proposition for search and listing preview.
- Full description: 4000 characters. Readable support copy with natural keyword themes.
- Screenshots: conversion assets; show real app value, not decorative marketing.
- Feature graphic: 1024x500; important for promotional surfaces.
- Preview video: make the first 30 seconds clear; autoplay is often muted/capped.
- Custom store listings: up to 50; use for country, campaign, keyword-intent, and re-engagement variants.
- Store listing experiments: test one major asset at a time, run long enough, judge by acquisition and retention.
- Ratings/reviews: trust and ranking signal. Reply and mine complaints for product fixes.
- Android vitals: crashes, ANRs, battery, startup, and app size affect growth and discoverability.
- Acquisition reporting: use Conversion analysis by source, country, language, search term, and UTM.

## Assessment Workflow

1. Read Play listing sources: `store/`, `metadata/`, `fastlane/metadata/android/`, Play exports, screenshots, feature graphic, release notes, app specs.
2. Identify target audience, core job-to-be-done, top 3 conversion claims, and top 3 keyword themes.
3. Check metadata against real app behavior and policy. Misleading claims are readiness risks.
4. Check first screenshots and feature graphic for concrete product value.
5. Check localization readiness: app UI, listing, screenshots, support/privacy pages.
6. Check Android vitals and app size before recommending paid scaling.
7. If keyword volume or competitor rank data is unavailable, mark it unverified. Do not invent volume.

## Metadata Guidance

- Avoid emojis, promotional claims, rankings claims, repetitive keywords, and unsupported superlatives.
- Use keyword themes naturally across title, short description, and full description.
- Do not add raw keyword lists to the description.
- Keep ad creative, custom listing copy, screenshots, and in-app experience aligned.
