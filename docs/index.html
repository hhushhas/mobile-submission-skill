Built for the messy middle

The part after the app is “done”

Mobile submission is not one command. It is native config, store policy, privacy declarations, review notes, builds, signing, console-only forms, and a final pre-submit check.

How it works

A release operator, not a checklist

01

Discover

Maps iOS, Android, backend, auth, permissions, assets, privacy URLs, and store metadata from the real repo.

02

Review

Runs readiness gates for blockers, high-risk issues, polish, and unknowns before submission work begins.

03

Prepare

Creates a structured packet, human console task list, artifact hashes, review notes, and secret references.

04

Execute

Delegates Apple and Google workstreams through CLI/API where possible, with dry-run safety by default.

05

Guide

For dashboard-only work, gives exact human instructions with field names, control types, and values.

06

Submit

Runs a final pre-submit gate, then moves to TestFlight, App Review, or Play track submission when approved.

Console work without wandering

Dashboard-only fields become guided tasks

Apple and Google still have forms that are better handled by a human. The skill generates targeted instructions instead of sending you into a console maze.

Google Play: Data Safety

  • PathPolicy > App content > Data safety
  • ControlYes/No + data-type flow
  • SelectNo data collected, when evidence supports it
  • Confirmgoogle data safety done

Optional Growth Pass

Release cleanly, then improve discovery

Store conversion

Audits screenshots, previews, app icon, title, subtitle, descriptions, feature graphic, ratings, and reviews.

ASO and discovery

Reviews Apple keywords, Google metadata, custom product pages, custom store listings, experiments, and vitals.

Paid acquisition

Plans Apple Ads, Google App campaigns, Meta, TikTok, creators, deep links, attribution, and test budgets.

Safety defaults

Designed to avoid accidental damage

  • Dry-run firstNo App Store Connect or Google Play mutation by default.
  • Secret refs onlyUses op://, env:, secret:, and ref: style references.
  • Final gateChecks packet, store state, human tasks, listing, privacy, and reviewer access before submission.
